PERFECT INriNITIVES: The perfect active infinitive is
made by substituting -isse for the third singular ending
-it. Thus:

paravísse—to have prepared
habuísse—to have had
cepísse—to have taken

The perfect infinitive passive is made by using the
infinitive "to be"—esse—with the proper form of the
perfect passive participle, e.g.:

interféctus esse—to have been killed.

Catilína dícitur interféctus esse—Catiline is said to
have been killed.

Multi dicüntur interfécti esse—Many are said to
have been killed.
